Event · December 14, 2023

We asked whether tech
is a promise or a burden.

ELP Foundation's first Mini Summit brought together entrepreneurs, researchers, and development professionals at our Magodo headquarters.

← Back to Blog

By ELP Foundation · December 2023

On December 14, 2023, ELP Foundation held its first Mini Summit at 41 CMD Road, Magodo, Lagos. The theme: Technology Tools for Small Businesses: A Promise or a Burden? It was a question we had been sitting with for four years of surveys, and we wanted to stop asking it quietly and start asking it in public.

The event brought together small business owners, researchers, NGO practitioners, and technology providers for a panel discussion, awards ceremony, presentations, and networking. Television cameras were present. The conversation was honest.

What the panel said

The panel discussed three things that our research had already shown privately but that needed to be said out loud. First: most digital tools for small businesses in Nigeria are built for medium-sized enterprises and retrofitted downward. The result is that they are too complicated, too expensive, or solve the wrong problems for the market trader or provisions seller who is the actual target user.

Second: training alone does not change behaviour. Our own data had shown this — firms that received only information about record-keeping tools adopted them at roughly half the rate of firms that also received direct consulting support. The panel agreed: you have to reduce the cost of adopting the tool, not just explain the benefit.

Third: the most important missing feature in every e-bookkeeping app reviewed was stock management. Not profit calculation, not tax compliance — stock. Because for a market trader, the question that matters most is not "what did I earn?" but "what do I have left and what do I need to buy?"

The Award of Excellence

At the Summit, ELP Foundation presented its first Award of Excellence to a community member who had demonstrated outstanding commitment to business improvement and knowledge-sharing among peers in Lagos markets. The moment — a woman holding the award with visible pride in front of the ELP banner — was one of the most photographed of the evening.

Awards are not something we do often. But this one mattered. It was a public statement that the work of running a small business honestly and well deserves recognition — not just from funders or researchers, but from the community itself.

What came next

The Mini Summit became a reference point for our 2026 programme design. The conversations confirmed our move toward EmpowerBot — a WhatsApp-based tool that removes the friction of app downloads entirely. They also shaped the LSETF Agege bootcamp structure: less lecture, more hands-on application, with standardised before-and-after tests to measure actual learning.

We plan to hold the next Summit in late 2026, after the EmpowerFarmers harvest data is in. The question that year will be different. But we will still be asking it in public.
