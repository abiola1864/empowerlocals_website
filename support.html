Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know

100% of your contribution purchases agricultural inputs for one enrolled farmer — certified seeds, NPK fertilizer, neem-coated urea, herbicide, and a labour cash grant. ELP Foundation's operating costs are covered separately. Nothing is deducted from your contribution.

It is a contribution, not a loan. You receive no financial return. At harvest, the farmer contributes grain back to the community fund — which supports the next farmer. Your contribution enables that cycle.

Our 2024 pilot recorded no crop failures across all 11 enrolled farmers. However, if a crop fails due to weather, disease, or circumstances outside the farmer's control, the farmer is expected to contribute half of their normal in-kind repayment where their harvest permits. If a total crop failure is documented by our field team, the farmer has no contribution obligation for that season. In that case, ELP Foundation absorbs the shortfall — this is capped at one farmer per donor per season.

We send a written confirmation with the farmer's name and community once inputs are delivered. You also receive a mid-season field update and a full harvest report between December 2026 and January 2027.

Yes. $60 funds a half-acre plot for one farmer. $120 funds a full acre — the recommended level of support. To fund multiple farmers, indicate this in the interest form or email abiola@empowerlocals.org directly.

We accept contributions in USD, GBP, and NGN. Bank transfer details are sent to you within 48 hours of completing the interest form.

The 2026 planting window opens in May. Harvest is expected in October 2026. We need confirmed contributions by 31 May 2026.

Yes. Empowerment for Local People Foundation is a registered non-governmental organisation in the Federal Republic of Nigeria (Corporate Affairs Commission registration).
