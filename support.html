Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know

100% of your contribution goes directly to purchasing certified seeds, fertilizer, herbicide, and a labour cash grant for one farmer. ELP Foundation's operating costs are covered separately. No overhead is deducted from your contribution.

It is a contribution, not a loan. You do not receive financial returns. At harvest, the farmer contributes 5 mudus (~5.65 kg) of grain back to the community fund — which supports the next farmer. Your contribution enables that cycle.

No harvest has failed in our pilot (2024: zero crop failures across 11 farmers). However, if a crop fails due to weather or disease outside the farmer's control, there is no repayment obligation on the farmer. Your contribution is still fully deployed.

We send a confirmation message with the farmer's name and community once inputs are delivered. We also send a mid-season field update and a harvest report after October 2026.

Yes. Each $60 (~N84,600) funds one half-acre package. Email abiola@empowerlocals.org if you want to fund a group or a whole community.

We accept contributions in USD, GBP, and NGN. Transfer details are provided after you complete the interest form.

The 2026 planting window opens in May 2026. Harvest is expected October 2026. We need confirmed contributions by mid-May.

Yes. Empowerment for Local People Foundation is a registered non-governmental organisation in the Federal Republic of Nigeria (CAC registration).
