Who we are

From Lagos markets
to Niger State farms.

Founded in 2017 and registered in 2018, the Empowerment for Local People Foundation began with a simple question: why do small businesses in Lagos fail even when their owners work harder than anyone? The answer was rarely effort. It was access — to knowledge, to technology, to the right tools at the right time.

Eight years later, we have trained over 8,000 small businesses, supported 15 farmers in Minna without any collateral, launched an AI-powered mentoring tool on WhatsApp, and partnered with the Lagos State Employment Trust Fund to measure what entrepreneurs actually learn — not just whether they attended.

We are not just building capacity. We are building evidence.

Our milestones

A timeline of impact

2017

Foundation

Established with a vision to empower micro-businesses in Nigeria. First volunteer training delivered in Lagos.

2018

Official Registration

Registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission as a limited-by-guarantee trustee. Formal operations begin.

2019

First Major Programme

Launched comprehensive training series. Built a network of 30+ volunteers across Lagos.

2022

EmpowerBot Launched

Released Nigeria's first AI-powered WhatsApp mentoring tool for small business owners.

2023

LSETF Partnership

Partnered with the Lagos State Employment Trust Fund. Began measuring actual learning outcomes, not just attendance.

2024

EmpowerFarmers Pilot

Supported 11 smallholder farmers in Minna with zero-collateral inputs. 100% programme completion. Average yield tripled.

2026

Scale Up

250+ entrepreneurs trained in Agege bootcamp. 100-farmer target set for Minna. Evidence lab embedded with NCC and CBN.

Our beneficiaries have an average age of 36. 58% are women. A median daily profit of N20,000. They are not statistics. They are the economy.
